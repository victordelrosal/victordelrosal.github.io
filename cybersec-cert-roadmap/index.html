What to Avoid

CEH (€874-2,500+)
Overpriced, multiple-choice, theoretical. eJPT teaches more practical skill at 1/5th the cost. Only worthwhile if employer-funded.
SANS/GIAC (€8,700+ each)
Excellent but a single cert exceeds the entire 24-month budget. Employer-funded only. Never self-fund as a graduate.
CISSP (premature)
Requires 5 years' experience. Listing it as a grad signals misalignment with the field. Study at year 3-4; sit the exam when qualified.
Google Cyber Certificate
Good learning tool, not a hiring credential. Irish recruiters don't treat it as equivalent to Security+. A CS grad already exceeds its scope.

AI + Security: The Differentiator

SC-200 already covers AI
April 2026 update includes Microsoft Security Copilot integration: AI-augmented triage, KQL query generation, automated investigation. No separate AI cert needed at entry level.
CompTIA SecAI+ (Feb 2026)
First vendor-neutral AI+security cert. Requires 3-4 years' experience. Mid-career target, not entry-level. Covers AI threat detection and SOAR automation.
CAISP (Practical DevSecOps, €1,241)
Hands-on AI security: OWASP LLM Top 10, MITRE ATLAS, MLOps pipeline security. 6-hour practical exam. Not yet in Irish postings, but strong differentiator once employed.
ISC2 AI Integration
ISC2 integrated AI concepts across all exam domains in April 2026. CC and CISSP candidates now encounter AI-security content automatically.

The NIS2 Opportunity

Ireland's biggest structural tailwind in cybersecurity history

Ireland is expanding from ~450 regulated entities (NIS1) to 4,500-6,000 under NIS2. The NCSC's CyberFundamentals (CyFun) framework will become the national assessment scheme, with self-registration expected July 2026 and first audits mid-2027. ISO 27001 covers approximately 70% of NIS2 requirements. Fines run up to €10 million or 2% of global turnover. Candidates who combine technical certs (Security+, SC-200) with GRC credentials (ISO 27001, eventually CISM) sit at the intersection of the two largest hiring waves in Irish tech.
