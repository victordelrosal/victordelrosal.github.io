# guacluv — Business Architecture

> **Avocados, with love.** Perfectly-ripened avocados and craft guac kits, timed to the exact day you want to eat them.
> Live: **https://victordelrosal.com/guacluv**

*A concept venture architected end-to-end. This document is the operating blueprint: what we sell, to whom, how the money works, and how we grow.*

---

## 1. The problem

The avocado is the most-loved fruit of the modern kitchen — and the most frustrating. It is either **rock-hard** when you need it or **brown inside** the moment you cut it. Shoppers gamble every single time. Retailers bin ~1 in 3 avocados to spoilage. The one thing nobody has solved is **timing**.

## 2. The insight

People don't want "an avocado." They want **a ripe avocado on the specific day they plan to eat it** — Taco Tuesday, Sunday brunch, the dinner party Friday. Ripeness is a *scheduling* problem, not a *supply* problem. Own the schedule and you own the category.

## 3. The product: a ripeness-as-a-service brand

guacluv sells precision-ripened avocados against a **ripe-day guarantee**: tell us when you'll eat, we ripen to hit peak on that date, or the next one is free.

## 4. Market

- **Category size:** global avocado market ~US$18–20B and growing double digits; Europe is the fastest-growing import region.
- **Beachhead:** Ireland (Dublin, Cork, Galway) — high avocado consumption, dense delivery geography, underserved by premium fresh DTC. Then UK, then EU metros.
- **Target customer:** the "avocado-forward" household — 25–45, health-conscious, brunch-and-toast culture, already paying a premium for quality and convenience, already subscribing to other DTC food (coffee, meal kits).

## 5. Positioning

> Everyone sells avocados. **Only guacluv sells *ripe* — on your day.**

- vs. **supermarket**: they sell a gamble; we sell a guarantee.
- vs. **grocery delivery**: they sell speed; we sell *timing*.
- vs. **meal kits**: they sell dinner; we sell the one ingredient people most often ruin.

Brand voice: warm, playful, a little cheeky ("Never gamble on an avocado again"), but credible on sourcing and sustainability.

## 6. How it works (ops)

1. **Source** — direct partnerships with regenerative growers; buy pre-climacteric (unripened) fruit for maximum ripening control and shelf life.
2. **Ripen** — climate-controlled ripening rooms (temperature + ethylene + humidity) staged so each batch hits peak on its target delivery date. This is the core IP and moat.
3. **Grade & pack** — firmness-tested (near-IR / manual QC), packed in moulded-pulp + home-compostable wrap.
4. **Deliver** — carbon-neutral last-mile in the beachhead cities; offset courier for wider EU.
5. **Guarantee** — photo-based replacement flow for any avocado off-peak; the data feeds back into ripening models.

## 8. Go-to-market

**Phase 0 — Waitlist (now).** Landing page at victordelrosal.com/guacluv captures founding members. Scarcity ("first 500 lock in pricing for life") + referral loop ("share the luv, you both get a free kit").

**Phase 1 — Founding batch (launch).** Invite the waitlist by city as delivery density allows. Hand-hold ops, prove the ripe-day guarantee, gather ripening data.

**Phase 2 — Growth.** Referral engine + content (recipes, "avocado school"), micro-influencer seeding, café/gym partnerships as both wholesale channel and brand billboard.

**Phase 3 — Expand.** New cities, gifting (kits), seasonal single-origin drops, and B2B wholesale as a volume/margin stabiliser.

## 10. Moats

1. **Ripening data & models** — every order (and every guarantee claim) sharpens the schedule-to-peak engine. Compounding, proprietary, hard to copy.
2. **Grower relationships** — direct, regenerative, fair-price supply that competitors can't just buy on the spot market.
3. **Brand** — guacluv owns "ripe on your day." Warmth + guarantee = trust, and trust is the whole game in fresh food.
4. **Subscription lock-in** — habit + Club + referral rewards raise switching cost.

## 11. Risks & mitigations

- **Perishability / spoilage** → precision ripening + wholesale outlet for off-spec fruit + demand forecasting from subscriptions.
- **Last-mile cost** → route density via city-by-city rollout; offset courier only where density is thin.
- **Supply shocks (weather, price)** → multi-origin sourcing, seasonal single-origin storytelling turns variability into a feature.
- **Copycats** → data moat + brand + grower lock-in; move fast on the ripe-day guarantee as the ownable promise.
