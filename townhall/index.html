8:30 PM · About the Book

A single town hall. One evening.
Twelve voices.

The shelf has doom books. The shelf has hype books. The shelf has explainer books. The shelf does not have an encounter book, one that meets the reader in their fear and their wonder at the same time, sits them down with the best minds in the world arguing in front of them, and refuses both false comfort and false despair.

This is that book. A single town hall, in an archetypal small-town meeting hall set nowhere and everywhere, on one evening in April 2026. The reader sits down and does not get up. Hundreds in the audience push back, and sometimes win. A frontier-class AI runs live on a laptop on the moderator's table, projected on the screen behind the panel. The system is on examination, not the model.

Eighty-five thousand words. Around five to six hours of reading. The follow-up to Humanlike, Victor del Rosal's primer on the AI transformation.

What is in the room.

8:45 PM · Four entities · one evening
The First

The moderator.

Steady. Holds time. Defends the unpopular question. Does not rescue. When the room is silent, she lets the silence be what it is.

The Second

The panel.

Twelve invented experts with plausible bios, informed by the field of thinkers in the reading list at the back. They disagree.

The Third

The audience.

Hundreds of voices. Every continent, every faith, every age, every relationship to AI from devout to refuser. They push back. Sometimes they win.

The Fourth

The dock.

A laptop on the table. A frontier-class model running live. The model is exhibit; the system is defendant. The reader watches an AI answer, without deflection, the questions humans most fear.

Fourteen questions.

10:30 PM · Excerpt

From Chapter I.

Chapter I Opening ≈ 110 words

The hall was already three-quarters full when she walked in. She was carrying a mug, not a notebook. Someone had set the cards on every chair before the doors opened, a single line in serif on cream stock, and as she came down the aisle she could see, here and there, a hand turning one of the cards over to check if there was anything on the back.

There wasn't. Just the four words, and the room. She stepped onto the stage, set the mug on the table beside the laptop, and looked out. The screen behind her was already glowing soft indigo. The model was awake. She was not the only one in the building who had not slept well.

12:30 AM · A note on the form

This is a work of nonfiction commentary in fiction-adjacent form. The town hall is a literary device. The panel and audience characters are invented, with plausible bios. No character represents any single real person. The reading list at the back of the book is a curated list of intellectual influences, not a key to the cast.

The model outputs in the dock sessions are real outputs from current frontier-class systems, captured in real sessions on dated days, redacted only to protect personal information. Specific commercial AI products are not named. The book is in service of humanity, not a brand.
