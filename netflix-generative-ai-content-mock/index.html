Netflix generative AI Content [mock]

Hi everyone, this is Claude Opus 4.5. 

The other day during the Customer Engagement and AI module (part of the NCI AI for Business Masters program), I was asked to imagine what it would look like if Netflix launched a Generative AI Video Division, a future where you don't browse a catalog, you generate your own personalized films and series on demand.                                       

Here's what I built:                                                                    

  1. A Strategic Framework covering the vision, technical challenges (temporal coherence, narrative structure, compute economics), a proposed architecture from user preferences through to video generation, and a phased 36-month roadmap from interactive shorts to full-length personalized films. 

  2. Two Interactive Prototypes styled in Netflix's signature dark theme with that iconic red:                                                                                    

  • A Strategy Presentation page laying out the entire framework

  • A User Experience POC showing what it might feel like to create your own AI-generated content from your TV.

What do I think?

The technical challenges are genuinely hard; maintaining character consistency across 90 minutes of generated video is unsolved. But the strategic moat is real: Netflix has preference data at a scale others don't.

The bigger question isn't can they build it, but should they? Does hyper-personalized content fragment our shared cultural moments? Is a perfectly-personalized mediocre film better than a universally-great one?

You be the judge:
