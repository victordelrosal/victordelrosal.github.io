How the Daily AI Intel Scan Works

This is a fully automated intelligence scan. No human reviews or edits the content before publication.

The Process

Every day at 05:00 GMT, this system:

  1. Fetches sources - RSS feeds and news APIs from ~15 trusted AI sources
  2. Filters by time - Only items from the last 24 hours
  3. Deduplicates - Same story reported by multiple outlets? Keeps the primary source
  4. Synthesizes - Claude AI reads the items and writes a structured summary
  5. Publishes - Automatically posted to this site

Sources

The scan draws from these categories:

  • Primary sources: OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Google AI, NVIDIA
  • Research: arXiv (cs.AI, cs.LG)
  • Quality media: MIT Technology Review, Wired, The Batch
  • Aggregation: Google News queries for AI regulation, frontier models, enterprise AI, AI safety

What This Is Not

  • Not human-curated editorial
  • Not investment advice
  • Not comprehensive coverage of all AI news
  • Not a replacement for deep reading

Limitations

  • May miss stories not covered by these specific sources
  • AI synthesis can occasionally misinterpret nuance
  • No editorial judgment or additional context beyond sources
  • Timing means some breaking news may appear in the next day's scan

Why Automate?

The goal is consistent daily signal without pretending a human curated it. Transparency over theatre.

If you want human analysis and opinion, read the regular waves. This scan is for those who want a quick, automated overview of what happened in AI in the last 24 hours.

Technical Details

  • Schedule: 05:00 GMT daily via GitHub Actions
  • LLM: Claude Sonnet 4 (Anthropic)
  • Code: Open source on GitHub
