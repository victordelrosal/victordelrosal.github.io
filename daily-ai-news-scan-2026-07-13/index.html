AI Safety Index reveals poor scores from major AI labs

Daily AI News Scan — Monday, July 13, 2026

Executive Summary

The newly released AI Safety Index assigned failing grades to the industry's leading labs, with Anthropic earning C+ and OpenAI and Google DeepMind each receiving C — a signal that self-regulatory commitments remain unmatched by measurable outcomes. In parallel, Apple filed suit against OpenAI and former employees over alleged trade secret theft tied to hardware development, escalating legal tensions across the sector. Meta's rapid discontinuation of its Muse Image feature after privacy and talent-rights objections underscores the continued fragility of public trust in generative AI products.

Top Stories

1. AI Safety Index reveals poor scores from major AI labs

The AI Safety Index delivered low grades to the field's most prominent developers: Anthropic received a C+, while OpenAI and Google DeepMind each received a C. The results suggest that despite public commitments to responsible AI, independent evaluation finds significant gaps in measurable safety practices across the board. The release coincided with separate reporting that Google and OpenAI sold advanced AI models to Singapore-based subsidiaries of Chinese companies that the US Pentagon has blacklisted over alleged military ties — adding a geopolitical dimension to the safety discourse. GitHub also moved to address a related risk vector, adding prompt-injection detection capabilities to CodeQL for JavaScript and TypeScript flows that connect to OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google GenAI SDKs.

3. Meta discontinues Muse Image after privacy backlash

Meta pulled its Muse Image feature from Instagram within days of launch after users and talent organizations, including SAG-AFTRA, objected to the feature's automated generation of AI likenesses from public Instagram accounts. The swift reversal reflects growing sensitivity around consent and image rights in generative AI, particularly when public social media profiles are used as training or reference data without explicit opt-in. The episode follows a pattern of AI product launches colliding with user and advocacy group resistance, forcing rapid course corrections. Meta's willingness to withdraw the feature quickly may signal a more cautious posture ahead of anticipated regulatory scrutiny in key markets.
