Anthropic releases Claude Sonnet 5 as cheaper model for running agents

Daily AI News Scan ��� Thursday, July 2, 2026

Executive Summary

Anthropic dominates today's news cycle with the launch of Claude Sonnet 5, a cost-optimized agentic model priced at $2/$10 per million tokens through August 2026, now serving as the default for Free and Pro users. Separately, Anthropic released Claude Science, a specialized research workbench for scientists with native support for protein and chemical structure rendering. Google also entered the image generation efficiency race with Nano Banana 2 Lite, capable of producing images in four seconds at under four cents per thousand — underscoring the industry's accelerating push toward cheaper, faster inference.

Top Stories

1. Anthropic releases Claude Sonnet 5 as cheaper model for running agents

Anthropic launched Claude Sonnet 5, positioning it as a cost-effective alternative to Opus 4.8 for agentic workloads including planning, tool use, coding, and longer-horizon tasks. Promotional pricing is set at $2 per million input tokens and $10 per million output tokens through August 31, 2026, making it the new default model for Free and Pro tier users. The model achieves 63.2% on SWE-bench Pro for coding tasks, though it underperforms Sonnet 4.6 on cybersecurity benchmarks such as CyberGym — a reminder that capability trade-offs remain task-dependent. The release reflects Anthropic's broader strategy of tiering models by cost and capability to lower the barrier for production agentic deployments.

Source: Wired AI

2. Claude Science AI workbench for scientists now available

Anthropic released Claude Science in beta, a dedicated AI workbench for scientific research available on macOS and Linux. The application natively renders protein and chemical structures, integrates with over 60 optional scientific database connectors, and includes workflow layers connecting databases, sub-agents, and fact-checking mechanisms. Code-traced artifacts and on-demand compute environments are also part of the feature set, targeting researchers who need reproducible, verifiable outputs. The launch signals Anthropic's intent to move beyond general-purpose AI assistants into domain-specific research infrastructure.

Source: The Rundown AI

3. Google launches Nano Banana 2 Lite for rapid AI image generation

Google released Nano Banana 2 Lite, its fastest and most cost-efficient image generation model under the Gemini API, producing images in approximately four seconds at under four cents per thousand images. The launch was paired with the availability of Gemini Omni Flash for developer-facing video generation and conversational editing capabilities. The pricing and speed metrics position the model for high-volume creative testing and iterative workflows where cost-per-generation matters. Google's simultaneous movement on both image and video generation tools reflects continued competitive pressure in the multimodal inference market.

Source: TechCrunch AI
