Top AI CEOs want America to lead the world

Daily AI News Scan — Friday, June 19, 2026

Executive Summary

At the G7 summit, Anthropic's Dario Amodei and Google DeepMind's Demis Hassabis pushed for a U.S.-led coalition to govern global AI rules, chips, and safety standards — while separately, leaked communications revealed Anthropic's ongoing conflict with U.S. export restrictions on its Mythos and Fable models. In a significant talent shift, Transformer co-author and former Gemini co-lead Noam Shazeer joined OpenAI ahead of its IPO. Alibaba also entered the physical AI space with its Qwen Robot Suite, releasing three specialized foundation models targeting robotics navigation, manipulation, and world modeling.

Top Stories

1. Top AI CEOs want America to lead the world

Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei and Google DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis used the G7 summit in France to advocate for a U.S.-led international coalition governing AI model access, chip exports, and frontier safety risks. Amodei separately published a blog calling for mandatory pre-release testing, stronger security requirements, and coordinated global action on advanced AI. The push comes amid a parallel and escalating tension: newly leaked letters reveal Anthropic is still locked in conflict with the U.S. government over export restrictions on its Mythos and Fable models. Senior Trump administration officials were also reported to be exploring government equity stakes in major AI companies, through mechanisms such as "Trump Accounts" or a sovereign wealth fund structure.

Source: Wired AI

2. Google Gemini co-lead leaves for OpenAI

Noam Shazeer, co-author of the seminal "Attention Is All You Need" paper and technical co-lead of Google's Gemini project, has joined OpenAI to lead architecture research. The move is a significant talent acquisition for OpenAI as it approaches its IPO, pulling one of the field's most credentialed researchers directly from its primary competitor. Shazeer's background spans the foundational architecture underpinning nearly all modern large language models, making this a symbolic as well as operational win for OpenAI. Google loses not just an executive but a figure closely associated with the identity of its flagship AI product.

Source: TechCrunch AI

3. Alibaba Unveils Qwen-Robot Suite for Physical World AI

Alibaba released the Qwen Robot Suite, a set of three specialized foundation models targeting embodied AI: Qwen-RobotManip for object manipulation, Qwen-RobotNav for navigation, and Qwen-RobotWorld, a language-conditioned video world model that uses natural language as a unified action interface across robotics, driving, and embodied domains. The release signals Alibaba's intent to compete in physical AI infrastructure, a space also being pursued by major U.S. labs and hardware manufacturers. The use of natural language as a universal control interface across diverse physical tasks is a notable architectural choice. This positions Qwen not just as a language model family but as a broader platform for real-world agent deployment.

Source: The Rundown AI
