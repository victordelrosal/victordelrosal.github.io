Anthropic published research identifying an internal processing space inside Claude — dubbed J-Space — that mirrors the global workspace theory of human consciousness, marking a significant moment in AI interpretability research. Top Stories

1. Anthropic's J-lens uncovers Claude's internal workspace matching consciousness theory

Anthropic researchers identified a hidden internal space within Claude's neural architecture, termed J-Space, where concepts are held, edited, and manipulated before surfacing in model outputs. Using a technique called the Jacobian lens (J-lens), the team found this workspace functions as a private scratchpad — separating background processing from intentional reasoning in a manner consistent with global workspace theory, a prominent model of human conscious cognition. The research claims Claude can modulate thoughts, engage in multi-step problem-solving, and distinguish deliberate from automatic processes within this space. If the findings hold up to scrutiny, J-Space would represent one of the more concrete advances in mechanistic interpretability of large language models to date.

Source: The Verge AI

2. Tencent releases Apache-licensed Hy3 295B MoE model beating larger rivals

Tencent released Hy3, a 295-billion-parameter mixture-of-experts model that activates only 21 billion parameters at inference time, achieving strong performance on agentic search benchmarks while reducing hallucination rates from 12.5% to 5.4% compared to prior baselines. The model is available on Hugging Face and OpenRouter under an Apache 2.0 license, allowing commercial use, fine-tuning, and local deployment. Hy3 outperforms comparably sized open-source models and, by some benchmark measures, competes with larger closed frontier models. The release adds to a growing roster of capable open-weight Chinese models putting competitive pressure on proprietary Western offerings.

Source: The Rundown AI
